02
The Flow

Episode Structure

0 – 5 min
Cold Open
The Hook - Why This Podcast Exists
Anisha opens with a real story - a homeowner who started construction without a structural engineer and paid for it later. Sets the emotional stakes before introductions. The question that anchors the whole episode: "What does it actually take to build a home right?"
Led by Anisha
5 – 15 min
Segment 1
Guest Introductions - Their Journey & Why Kshipra
Both guests share their story. Two rank holders from SJCE who took very different paths through the corporate world before coming together. What gap did they see in Bangalore's construction space? What was the thing corporate couldn't give them?
Prashanth + Ranjini - personal, honest, origin story
15 – 30 min
Segment 2
The Structural Truth - What Every Homeowner Must Know
Soil testing, foundation types, structural design decisions, load-bearing myths, curing, steel reinforcement. Real stories from real projects. The things people skip to save money and the long-term cost of those decisions. Ranjini adds the project management layer.
Prashanth leads - Ranjini adds operational context
30 – 45 min
Segment 3
The Process - From Plot to Possession
A complete walkthrough of how a residential construction project runs. BBMP approvals, BOQ, contractor selection, construction stages, site supervision, waterproofing, quality checks - the full truth no one tells first-time builders. Honest, specific, Bangalore-centric.
Ranjini leads - Prashanth adds structural checkpoints
45 – 58 min
Segment 4
Smart Homes & Automation - Plan It or Regret It
When do you plan for smart home systems - before construction starts or after keys are handed over? Conduit planning, wiring provisions, solar panels, EV charging readiness, what is actually worth the investment in 2025. India's smart home market is growing at 29% CAGR - homebuilders need to think ahead.
Both guests - Prashanth on structural provisions, Ranjini on practical ROI
58 – 70 min
Segment 5
Financing & The Budget Reality
Construction loan vs home loan. Staged disbursements. Real cost per sq ft in Bangalore 2025. The 15% buffer rule - is it enough? Hidden costs, cost overruns, tax benefits under Section 80C and 24, and the true price of choosing the cheapest contractor. Anisha shares her own perspective from the interior design side.
Anisha leads the questions - both guests answer honestly
70 – 75 min
Close
Rapid Fire + Where to Find Them
High-energy rapid fire round - no thinking allowed. Guests share how listeners can reach them. Anisha closes with her personal takeaway from the conversation and teases who's coming in Episode 2.
All three - keep the energy up

    Prashanth
  • Someone builds a G+1 house today, and 10 to 15 years later wants to add a second floor. What structural provisions need to be made at the time of original construction to keep that future option open? What does "future-ready" design actually mean in practice?
    Huge concern among Bangalore homebuilders - almost everyone thinks about this

  • Ranjini - when the structural engineer's specification conflicts with what the contractor says he has "always done," how do you handle that on site? Who wins, and how do you make sure the homeowner doesn't get caught in the middle?

Before You Arrive

How to Prepare

◈
Have one real story ready

The questions that will get the most listener traction are the ones backed by a real project - a real problem someone faced, a real cost they paid. Think of 2 to 3 stories from your actual work at Kshipra that you can share comfortably (names not required). The best ones involve a mistake that was made and what it cost.

◈
Think in real Bangalore numbers

The audience is Bangalore homebuilders. They want real numbers - actual cost per sq ft, real approval timelines, real budget overruns. The more specific and honest, the more the conversation will resonate. No need to hedge with "it depends" alone - give them a range and the factors that move it.

◈
Your unique angle

Prashanth - you have seen construction from the offshore global scale down to Bangalore residential. That contrast is powerful. Lean into it when it's relevant. Ranjini - your NICMAR background and academic experience means you think in systems and frameworks. Use that to give listeners tools, not just information.

◈
The format
  • Recorded in-person - conversational, not formal
  • The questions are a guide, not a script - go wherever the conversation takes you
  • Speak naturally - this is for a real audience, not a lecture
  • Disagreements or pushback between you both make for great radio - do not avoid them
  • Duration is approximately 75 minutes total
  • Kannada and English mix - whatever feels natural to you in the moment
